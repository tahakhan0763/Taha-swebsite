9th & 10th Semester (Thesis Project)– Autism Rehabilitation Center, 272250 Sq ft Site

For my final-year thesis, I chose to design an Autism Rehabilitation Center on a 50 kanal site, focusing on creating a safe, inclusive, and therapeutic environment for individuals on the autism spectrum. The project was rooted in extensive research on autism spectrum disorder (ASD), spatial sensitivities, behavioral therapy methods, and global case studies. My design integrated specialized spaces such as sensory therapy rooms, occupational and speech therapy units, inclusive classrooms, and residential zones for both short-term and long-term stays. I also incorporated open green spaces, therapy gardens, and calm outdoor courtyards to promote healing through nature and reduce sensory overload. The layout was carefully planned to prioritize ease of navigation, visual comfort, and controlled stimulation, with zones clearly separated for public, semi-private, and private use. The overall vision was to create a nurturing environment that supports holistic rehabilitation addressing educational, medical, emotional, and social development under one roof. This project was not only a design challenge but a deeply meaningful opportunity to explore how architecture can truly improve quality of life.

8th Semester (Mini Thesis)– Eco-Tourism Project, Thandyani (70 Kanal)

My mini-thesis involved research into eco-tourism principles and sustainable architectural practices. Based on this, I designed an eco-tourism destination in the scenic hills of Thandyani, Abbottabad. Spread across 70 kanals, the project included lodges, viewing decks, hiking trails, and nature-friendly facilities. My design focused on minimal environmental impact, passive techniques, and enhancing the tourist’s connection to nature.

7th Semester– Bus Terminal, Abbottabad (50 Kanal)

Starting with the study of intercity and intracity transportation hubs, I analyzed successful bus terminal designs. I then proposed a comprehensive bus terminal for Abbottabad on a 50 kanal site, accommodating passenger movement, ticketing, waiting areas, commercial spaces, and vehicular flows. The design ensured user convenience, efficient circulation, and future adaptability, reflecting both urban function and regional identity.

6th Semester– District Headquarter Hospital, Hangu (200 Kanal)

This semester's project focused on healthcare architecture. I began with an extensive study of District Headquarter Hospitals (DHQs) and their design guidelines. The final output was a 200 kanal hospital design for Hangu, incorporating emergency, OPD, inpatient, surgical, diagnostic, and admin blocks. I emphasized patient-centric design, circulation hierarchy, and environmental comfort, ensuring functional efficiency and accessibility.

5th Semester– Middle High School in Hangu (25 Kanal)

In the fifth semester, I studied the architectural and functional requirements of middle and high schools. My final design was a 25 kanal school campus in Hangu, responding to both pedagogical and contextual needs. It included classrooms, laboratories, administrative areas, sports grounds, and green spaces. My focus was on student movement, daylighting, and a learning-friendly environment through zoning and open layouts.
